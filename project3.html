Introduction
Design Great Assistant For Managing Smart Cities.
There are more and more IoT devices being built everywhere in our environment. The city management owner asks to have good tools for them. The challenge is how to manage these devices' datin place and is easy to use. My role is to start with research, study the original system, form the concept, make information architecture, and design UI flow and graphics.
Mission
To design an IoT city management platform that accommodates multiple levels of users with different tasks.
Challenge
Designing platform that accommodates multiple levels of users, devices, and usage scenarios is challenge.
My Role
Role
User research, Prototyping, Product strategy, UI/UX design, Implementation

Jan 2021 - Sep 2022

The Problem
Clarify what information is most important for the city
Target Audience
The target audience of original web console are including all kind of users. It is equal to there is no target audience. The first direction we point out is finding the true user for this web console. With some interview to the project managers in CHT. We figured out there are two kind of target audiences: one is the decision maker of city management, Another one is the operator.
Complex multi-level multi-user platform
This system must meet the needs of asset and operational management for administrators. As city-level smart pole management system, in addition to big datmanagement, there are also asset management of various units and usage scenario requirements managed by operators. The system must accommodate hierarchical structure, flexible user permissions, and variety of different usage scenarios.
The define
Hierarchy definition
In order to manage this multi-device, multi-role, and multi-level platform, I first defined the system architecture as follows: grouping of management units and grouping of managed devices. With this architecture as basis, it became quite clear and clear how to define the management of personnel and assets.
Smart Pole Operation Scenarios
We interviewed managers who have built urban street lighting systems and found that they have specific needs for street lighting management, such as: real-time notification of street light failures, management of large areof street lights at once, and understanding the equipment needs attached to street lights in real-time. We designed corresponding solutions for these scenarios.
The design
Wireframe & UI Flow
We discussed with the managers using wireframes and went through multiple iterations to come up with the user interface that we believe is most suitable for smart street light managers.
Design System
This is website that can be used on both computers and tablets. Considering that users will use the platform in different environments, the system is designed with both light and dark modes. Our team also designed the CHT CityOS corporate identity as the basis for developing the web design.
Final Design
This is large-scale system based on the perspective of urban governance. We have designed platform that meets the needs of city managers.
the END RESULT
Go to market
The CHT CityOS platform has been successfully exhibited in various smart cities and has been approached by multiple cities for use. The platform's architectural design has also influenced the website platform design within ChunghwTelecom, and more platforms will be developed based on this.
