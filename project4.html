iEnergy Console
Comprehensive Management Platform for Electric Vehicle Energy Networks
Introduction
iEnergy Console for Smart EV Infrastructure
iEnergy Console is a comprehensive web-based management platform designed for operators and administrators to monitor and manage electric vehicle battery swapping stations, energy assets, and operational data in real-time. This system provides a centralized hub for tracking station performance, battery health, revenue metrics, and customer service operations across entire energy networks.
Mission
Design a powerful yet intuitive back-office system that enables operators to efficiently manage complex EV energy infrastructure with real-time insights and actionable data.
Challenge
Balance information density with usability across multiple user roles (from technicians to executives) while ensuring critical alerts are immediately visible and actionable.
My Role
Lead UX/UI Designer
User research, Information architecture, Interaction design, Visual design, Design system

Oct 2019 - Sep 2022
The Problem
Managing Complex Data Across Multiple Stakeholders
Background
As electric vehicle adoption grows, the complexity of managing battery swapping infrastructure increases exponentially. Operators need to monitor hundreds of stations, thousands of batteries, and manage real-time events while ensuring service quality and revenue optimization. The original system lacked hierarchy, had poor information architecture, and failed to surface critical alerts effectively.
Key Challenges Identified
Multi-Level User Hierarchy
Different user roles (LV1 Admin, LV2 Staff, LV3 Operator) require different access levels and views of the same data.
Real-Time Critical Alerts
Battery swap failures and station outages must be immediately visible and actionable to prevent service disruption.
Complex Asset Management
Track individual battery health, station capacity, and equipment lifecycle across entire network infrastructure.
Revenue & Analytics
Provide actionable insights on swap patterns, revenue trends, and operational efficiency for strategic planning.
Research & Discovery
Understanding Operator Workflows
I conducted contextual inquiries with station operators, technical staff, and management teams to understand their daily workflows, pain points, and critical decision-making moments. Through shadowing sessions and interviews, I mapped out the information hierarchy and identified what data needs to be surfaced at different organizational levels.
Key Findings
1
Operators Need Contextual Dashboards
Different roles focus on different metrics. Technicians need battery-level details, while executives need high-level KPIs and trends.
2
Event History is Critical for Troubleshooting
Operators spend significant time tracking down the root cause of failures. Complete event logs with filtering and search capabilities are essential.
3
Geographic View Needed for Field Operations
When issues arise, operators need to quickly identify station locations and dispatch field teams with precise geographic information.
The Solution
Role-Based Dashboard with Real-Time Intelligence
I designed a multi-tiered dashboard system that surfaces the right information to the right users at the right time. The interface prioritizes critical alerts through visual hierarchy, color coding, and intelligent notifications, while providing deep-dive capabilities for detailed analysis when needed.
Design Process
Information Architecture & Wireframing
I started by mapping the information architecture to establish clear hierarchies and relationships between different data types. Working closely with engineers and operators, I created wireframes that prioritized critical information while maintaining flexibility for different user workflows.
Key Design Decisions
Dashboard Modularity
Designed modular dashboard cards that can be customized per user role, allowing admins to see revenue metrics while technicians focus on battery diagnostics.
Color-Coded Alert System
Implemented a three-tier alert system (Critical/Warning/Info) with distinct colors and visual treatments to ensure immediate recognition of priority issues.
Live Status Indicators
Added real-time status indicators throughout the interface, including animated "LIVE" badges and auto-refreshing data feeds to maintain situational awareness.
Design System & Visual Language
I developed a comprehensive design system inspired by modern tech dashboards with a professional, trustworthy aesthetic. The dark mode interface reduces eye strain for operators monitoring systems throughout the day, while the cyan accent color provides high contrast for critical information.

Key Learnings & Reflections
Context is Everything in B2B Design
Unlike consumer apps where simplicity often wins, enterprise dashboards need to surface complexity in an organized way. Operators told me they'd rather have information density than multiple clicks to find critical data.
Iterate with Real Users in Real Conditions
Testing the interface in actual operation centers revealed issues we never caught in the design studio — like glare on screens or the need for larger click targets when operators are under time pressure.
Design Systems Enable Scalability
Building a comprehensive design system upfront paid dividends as the product expanded. New features could be designed and developed 3x faster using established components and patterns.
